COVID-19 Information V1.0.3

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by a virus called SARS-CoV-2. It was first identified in Wuhan, China in late 2019.

Symptoms:

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell

Transmission:

The virus spreads primarily through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

Prevention:

  • Vaccination: Getting vaccinated and boosted is the best way to protect yourself and others.
  • Masks: Wearing a well-fitting mask in public indoor settings can help reduce transmission.
  • Handwashing: Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
  • Social distancing: Maintain at least 6 feet of distance from others.
  • Stay home when sick: If you are sick, stay home to avoid spreading the virus.

Reliable Information Sources:

  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)
  • U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Please consult with a healthcare professional for any medical concerns related to COVID-19.
